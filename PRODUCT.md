# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The Director of E-commerce — or the founder wearing that hat — at a $5–15M US consumer-product brand. Functional categories where shoppers ask multi-constraint "what should I buy" questions: home, kitchen, beauty, electronics, supplements, replacement parts.

They own the revenue number. They've seen `chatgpt.com` appear in their GA4 referrals, they suspect they're already losing sales inside ChatGPT, and they have no number — and no answer — when leadership asks "what's our ChatGPT plan?" They read on their phone, between meetings. They are pragmatic, ROI-literate, allergic to hype, and quietly anxious about being late the way some brands were late to Amazon. The objection is *how*, not *whether*.

## Product Purpose

Vektor10 is the first ChatGPT commerce agency. It gets consumer-product brands found, recommended, and bought inside ChatGPT, and proves it with a category-share number no one else produces. It does the work — feeds, prices, product data, the digital shelf — and is explicitly not another analytics dashboard.

The site's job is to convert that Director of E-commerce: make the invisible problem feel urgent and real, establish Vektor10 as the credible operator (not a dashboard, not a futurist), and drive the free-plan CTA. Success = booked free-plan conversations from qualified DTC brands. Public launch June 15, 2026.

## Brand Personality

Three forces held in deliberate tension — internal handle: **"warm instrumentation."**

- **Artisan / hand-touched.** VantaFive's customer-service DNA: a team that does the work, by hand, for you. Warm, human, considered. The opposite of a self-serve subscription.
- **Technical / instrument-grade.** Mathematically precise and genuinely innovating — the only ones measuring the new shelf at the SKU level. Monospace and technical motifs are *earned* (the company really is technical), never costume.
- **Approachable / lightly playful.** Speaks DTC, not academia. Doesn't take itself too seriously. Carries retro-consumer warmth, in step with the e-commerce clients it serves.

Voice: direct, specific, commerce-native, plainspoken, no hype. Emotional goals: **confidence + a little wonder.** A **dreamlike** quality (cinematic, nostalgic-yet-futuristic, liminal imagery) is a *required* emotional charge of this brand, delivered through asset selection — not optional decoration.

## Anti-references

- Generic GEO / AEO agencies and their "get cited in ChatGPT" sameness.
- AI-futurist consultancies ("the dawn of agentic commerce"). We are here-and-now, fix-it-this-quarter.
- The dashboard tools we position against (Profound, Peec): we are NOT another analytics dashboard you have to operate yourself.
- Faceless enterprise SaaS that reads as "an automated service at an upcharge."
- Both AI-slop reflexes: (1) dark-SaaS + teal-gradient + big-number-hero; (2) the "editorial-typographic restraint" lane (display-serif italic + mono labels + ruled lines + no imagery). Avoid both.
- StoryBrand-template clones ("Struggling with X? We get it. Here's our 3-step plan.").

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** We sell legibility-to-AI; the site must be the proof — fast, semantic, structured, crawlable. (It doubles as our best AEO asset.)
2. **We do the work, not a dashboard.** Every section should feel hand-built and human, never like a tool the visitor has to run themselves.
3. **Make the invisible visible.** The core value is seeing a shelf you can't see. Design should reveal and instrument the phenomenon, not just assert claims about it.
4. **Function before decoration.** Conventional, proven landing-page structure; clarity and fast information-finding come first. Effort is concentrated (hero + design system now; bespoke assets below the fold later).
5. **One intense moment beats five.** A committed point of view and a single wondrous hero moment over scattered effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast (watch muted text on the warm "bone" ground especially). Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states. A `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Mobile-first and fully responsive — the buyer reads on their phone.
